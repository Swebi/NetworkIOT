The single most impactful change: add ESP32 nodes + MQTT

Your paper says "distributed BLE scanning hubs" but right now everything runs on one Pi. Even two ESP32s turn this into a genuinely distributed system. Each ESP32 runs a small MicroPython or Arduino sketch that does BLE advertising scans and publishes raw MAC + RSSI payloads to a topic like ble/zone_A over MQTT. You run Mosquitto on the Pi as the broker. The Pi then subscribes to all zone topics and does the aggregation — the critical piece being cross-node de-duplication (if the same MAC appears at two nodes, count it once but assign it to the node with the strongest RSSI).

The gap that most hurts your paper's credibility: no ML

Your literature survey cites 10 papers on ML-enhanced sensing, but your system has none. The quickest fix is a simple time-series forecaster — collect a few days of history data from your JSON file, train a basic ARIMA or Prophet model on it, and show a 30-minute forecast line on the dashboard. Even a moving average with confidence bounds makes the "predictive analytics" claim true.

What completes the "alert system" claim: a Telegram bot

The python-telegram-bot library makes this trivial. When get_active_count() crosses a configured threshold, fire a message. This is maybe 20 lines of code on top of what you already have, but it makes the system useful and demo-able.

Dashboard v2: zone heatmap

Once you have multiple nodes, replace the single occupancy number with a grid/floorplan heatmap — one cell per zone, colour-coded by density. Streamlit's st.plotly_chart with a heatmap figure handles this cleanly. This is the most visually impressive upgrade for a demo or report.

Lowest effort, good for the paper: a REST API

Add a tiny FastAPI app alongside your Streamlit dashboard that serves /api/occupancy as JSON. This lets you claim the system supports third-party integration (smart HVAC, access control, etc.) which your paper already promises.

A realistic order to tackle these would be: MQTT + one ESP32 node → cross-node aggregator → Telegram alerts → zone heatmap → forecasting model → REST API. Which of these do you want to dig into first?
